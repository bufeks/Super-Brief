## 1. One-line ask

Design and execute a ¥15M launch + sustain campaign that establishes **HYPERBOOST RUN** — an ALPEN-exclusive SMU running shoe — as **"the most cushion & joy shoe for entry runners"** in Japan, with disproportionate attention to **women** and a measurable **traffic → conversion** lift in ALPEN stores and EC.

## 4. Target consumer (HYPERBOOST RUN at ALPEN)

**Core:** *Entry-Runner Millennials* (mid-20s to late-30s), with explicit instruction to **also reach women**.  
**Sub-target:** Potential Runners (lapsed / never-started).

| Dimension | Insight from brief |
|---|---|
| Running mindset | Active lifestyle; running is a *hobby*; aiming to **complete** a half or full marathon (not race it). |
| Running habits | Run for body-shape maintenance & lifestyle habit change. Motivation comes from **marathon-completion goals** and **community participation**. |
| Product behaviour | Looking for shoes that are **easy to run in** and **feel good**. Cushion is the #1 attribute they shop on. |

**Implications for creative & media:**
- Joy > Performance. The consumer is not searching for "fastest" or "lightest in class" — they are searching for "I can keep going."  
- **Community is the unlock.** Running clubs, group runs, finish-line moments are higher-resonance than solo hero shots.  
- Women-specific creative is *required*, not a bolt-on — likely a dedicated KV variant, casting balance, and influencer mix skewed female.

## 5. Competitive landscape

| Competitor | Price (¥) | Weight | Positioning |
|---|---|---|---|
| ASICS Novablast | 15–18k | 255 g | Energy-led |
| **On Cloudmonster** | 18–24k | 295 g | Max cushion |
| **Hoka Clifton** | 18–22k | 278 g | Max cushion |
| Nike Pegasus | 15–18k | 300 g | Daily workhorse |
| New Balance Fresh Foam | 15–20k | 260 g | Cushion |
| **HYPERBOOST RUN** | **19,800** | **247 g** | Cushion + Energy + **Lightest in set** |

**Strategic read:** the maximalist-cushion segment (Hoka, On) is heavy. HYPERBOOST RUN's defensible claim is **"max-class cushion at sub-250 g"** — the *lightest* shoe in its cushion peer group. Lead with the **weight-to-cushion ratio**, not raw stack height.

Against ASICS Novablast (closest weight, lower price), the wedge is **energy *plus* cushion** + Continental outsole as a durability/grip story.

## 6. Marketing mission & brief restatement

- **Mission:** Spread the JOY of running to **Potential / Entry** runners through HYPERBOOST.  
- **Objective (RUN/ALPEN):** Awareness + purchase lift; establish HB RUN as *"maximum-class cushion + joy of running"* shoe.  
- **Strategy:** Maximise the appeal of HYPERBOOST RUN.

**Stated challenges:**
1. ¥19,800 is **expensive for an entry shoe** → must justify added value.
2. Avoid cannibalising **EVO SL / Supernova**.
3. **Reach women.**

**Stated opportunities (from brief):**
- [+] Halo / shower effect from HYPERBOOST EDGE brand campaign.
- [+] Daily-wear / street silhouette unlocks non-run usage.
- [+] **Lighter** than competitors at similar stack height.
- [-] Summer launch — **heat-stroke risk** at any outdoor event.

**Mandated consideration:** A **Sept–Oct sustain phase** (Hokkaido Marathon, 31-Aug → race-week activation, is a natural anchor).

## 12. Strategic recommendations & open territories

These are research-driven angles for the response team — to be pressure-tested, not blindly executed.

### 12.1 Creative platform options

**A. "ハイパーに、軽やかに走ろう" / "Run hyper. Run light."**  
Lean into the *lightest-in-cushion-class* claim. Visual language: weightlessness, suspension, hovering footstrike. Anchors the cushion + light + bounce trinity in a single ownable idea.

**B. "Joy on every km" — community-led**  
Joy is the emotional payoff in the brief. Treat the campaign as a series of *finish moments* (the first 5k, the first half, the first morning run with a friend) rather than hero shots. Higher female resonance; easier to populate with adidas Runners Tokyo coaches; lower athlete dependency = lower risk of athlete-scheduling collapse.

**C. "Boost into the everyday"**  
Lifestyle bridge. Plays into the "daily-wear silhouette" opportunity called out in the brief. Risk: dilutes the running positioning if not balanced — recommended as a **secondary** layer for retargeting, not the primary KV.

> **Recommendation:** Lead with **B** (Joy / community) as the master narrative, use **A** (Run hyper / light cushion) as the product RTB layer, hold **C** in reserve for sustain-phase social.

### 12.2 Women-specific approach
- Casting at minimum 50/50; ensure female lead in master KV cutdown.
- Influencer mix: female runners + lifestyle / wellness creators (not race-elite).
- Mechanic idea: women's-only morning run from ALPEN TOKYO storefront, pre-open, coached by adidas Runners.
- Avoid pink-it-and-shrink-it — same product, same RRP, framed around running-as-self-care narrative.

### 12.3 In-store / SMU narrative
- The fact that HB RUN is **ALPEN-exclusive** is a sales argument the brief itself does not exploit hard enough. Treat the SMU as a *destination product* — "only at ALPEN" — across KV copy, EC LP, and in-store signage.
- Power-zone fixture brief (to separate vendor) should emphasise **lift-and-feel** — the weight surprise (sub-250 g with max cushion) is a tactile demo, not a visual one.

### 12.4 Sustain phase — Hokkaido Marathon (30 Aug)
- Race-week activation in Sapporo with adidas Runners coaches + ALPEN local store.
- Finisher content captured in late August feeds Sept/Oct social and ALPEN Magazine editorial.
- Bridges the brief's stated wish to "use the Hokkaido Marathon" without the agency owning the marathon itself.

### 12.5 Heat / safety mitigation (Jul–Aug)
- Move outdoor event sessions to **early-morning / pre-open** windows (already favoured by ALPEN's own activation rules — no extra cost).
- Pivot any midday consumer-facing activation **indoors** (ALPEN TOKYO ground floor / Unika Vision adjacent space).
- Provide hydration + cooling kit budget line (~¥300k) in any outdoor build.
